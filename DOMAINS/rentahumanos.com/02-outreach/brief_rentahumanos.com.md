# Campaign Brief: rentahumanos.com

## Domain Overview
- **Domain:** rentahumanos.com
- **Niche:** Spanish-Language Gig Economy / On-Demand Labor Platform
- **Target Market:** Freelance marketplaces, temporary staffing platforms, gig economy startups targeting Spanish-speaking markets
- **Value Prop:** "Renta Humanos" = "Rent Humans" in Spanish. A provocative, memorable domain for an on-demand labor platform. Edgy enough to stand out in a crowded gig economy space — it's the domain that starts conversations. For a freelance marketplace, staffing platform, or gig app serving the Spanish-speaking world, this domain has the kind of disruptive energy that builds brands.

## Messaging Strategy
- **Primary Angle:** The Disruptive Gig Brand. "Rent Humans" is provocative — it grabs attention and starts conversations. For a gig platform, that edge is a feature: it signals that you're doing something different from the corporate staffing incumbents. The domain is edgy enough for a startup, clear enough for a marketplace.
- **Key Talking Points:** Provocative, memorable, conversation-starting. "Renta" is action-oriented — direct call to action. Companion to rentapersona.com (warmer, more personal framing). 12 characters — reasonable for a descriptive domain.

## Tone & Cultural Considerations
- **Tone (Spanish):** Bold, disruptive, startup-energy. "Renta Humanos" has an edge — don't soften it.
- **Key insight:** The provocative framing ("rent humans") acknowledges the gig economy's industrial reality while owning it as a brand. It's honest in a way that sanitized corporate branding isn't.

## Objection Handling
- **"'Rent Humans' sounds dehumanizing":** "It's intentionally provocative — and that honesty resonates with gig workers who feel dehumanized by corporate platforms anyway. The domain owns the reality rather than hiding from it. That authenticity builds trust."
- **"Too edgy for corporate clients?":** "Startups win by being bold. Corporate clients ultimately care about results, not brand names. And 'Renta Humanos' is impossible to forget — that's marketing gold."
