## Tone & Cultural Considerations
- **Tone (English):** Professional, culturally-aware, direct. Show that you understand the Hispanic market opportunity — don't just pitch a domain, pitch the demographic strategy.
- **Tone (Spanish):** Warm, respectful, business-professional. Address decision-makers with formal titles (Sr., Sra., Lic.). Avoid US-centric assumptions about Latin American markets.
- **Cultural note:** "Aseguranza" is specifically a Spanish word — only pitch to companies that already serve or plan to serve Spanish-speaking customers. English-only insurers won't see the value.
- **Best approach:** Lead in English for US-based execs. For LATAM-based leads, open in Spanish — it shows respect and cultural understanding.
- **Key cultural insight for US Hispanic market:** Don't treat "Hispanic" as a monolith — Mexican-American, Cuban-American, Puerto Rican, and Central American communities have different insurance needs and cultural reference points.
