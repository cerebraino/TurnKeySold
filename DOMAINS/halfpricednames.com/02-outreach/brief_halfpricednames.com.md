- **Target Market:** Domain investors, startups seeking brandable domains, domain marketplaces

## Messaging Strategy
- **Primary Angle:** The Built-In Value Proposition. Most domain marketplaces need to explain their positioning. "Half Priced Names" does it in the domain itself. It's the perfect brand for a discount tier, a clearance marketplace, or a budget-friendly domain platform.
- **Key Talking Points:** Self-describing value proposition — no explanation needed. Perfect for a marketplace tier or standalone brand. "Half Priced" implies quality at a discount — not cheap, but smart. Three words that tell the complete business model.
- **Use cases:** SquadHelp or BrandBucket could launch "Half Priced Names" as a clearance tier. Namecheap or GoDaddy could use it for a budget domain marketplace. A new entrant could build an entire brand around it.

## Tone & Cultural Considerations
- **Tone:** Direct, value-focused, no-nonsense. Domain investors and marketplace operators are sophisticated buyers — focus on the business case, not the emotion.
- **Key insight:** The domain industry runs on positioning. "Half Priced Names" is a positioning that no current marketplace owns. It's a category-creating domain in a category that understands domain value better than anyone.

## Objection Handling
- **"We focus on premium — 'half priced' dilutes our brand":** "That's exactly why you'd want it as a separate brand. 'Half Priced Names' attracts a different buyer segment without touching your premium positioning. It's market expansion, not dilution."
- **"We already have a marketplace brand":** "Every major marketplace has tiered offerings — Amazon has Amazon Warehouse, Nordstrom has Nordstrom Rack. halfpricednames.com is your outlet tier — same quality domains, accessible price points."
- **"The domain industry doesn't need another marketplace":** "The domain aftermarket is growing rapidly. 'Half priced' is a positioning no one owns yet. This domain lets you claim it before someone else does."
