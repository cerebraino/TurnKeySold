# DomainMatch Pro Outreach Playbook: Premium Domain Sales

This playbook outlines the systematic approach for identifying, contacting, and closing buyers for premium domain names.

---

## 1. Multi-Channel Outreach Strategy

We employ a "Human-First, Automation-Supported" strategy. While we use automation for scale, every initial touchpoint must feel personalized and researched.

### Primary Channel: Email
*   **Tooling:** Cold email outreach platforms (e.g., Instantly, Lemlist) - *to be integrated*.
*   **Focus:** Direct, professional, and value-oriented.
*   **Best For:** Decision-makers (CEO, Founders, CMOs).

### Secondary Channel: LinkedIn
*   **Usage:** Used for multi-touch attribution. If a prospect doesn't respond to email, we "bump" them on LinkedIn.
*   **Focus:** Relationship building and social proof.
*   **Tactic:** Connection request without a pitch, followed by a soft inquiry if accepted.

---

## 2. Buyer Personas & Messaging Templates

### A. The Visionary (Founder/CEO)
**Focus:** Brand authority, category leadership, and long-term asset value.
*   **Template - Initial Hook:**
    *   *Subject:* [Domain.com] / [Company Name]
    *   *Body:* "Hi [Name], I've been following [Company Name]'s growth in the [Industry] space. I'm reaching out because I'm representing the owner of **[Domain.com]**, and given your position as a leader in [Niche], this asset seems like a natural fit for your brand's next phase. A category-defining domain like this doesn't just improve SEO; it cements trust instantly. Are you open to a brief chat about how this could fit into your long-term strategy?"

### B. The Strategist (Marketing/CMO)
**Focus:** Campaign efficiency, brand recall, and direct traffic.
*   **Template - Initial Hook:**
    *   *Subject:* Increasing [Company Name] brand recall with [Domain.com]
    *   *Body:* "Hi [Name], I noticed your recent campaign around [Topic]. Have you considered how **[Domain.com]** could simplify your user journey and lower your CAC? Shorter, keyword-exact domains typically see higher CTRs in ads and better organic performance. We're currently entertaining offers for the domain and wanted to see if it aligns with your Q3/Q4 marketing roadmap."

### C. The Opportunist (Investor/Strategic)
**Focus:** Resale value, defensive acquisition, or portfolio synergy.
*   **Template - Initial Hook:**
    *   *Subject:* Strategic acquisition opportunity: [Domain.com]
    *   *Body:* "Hi [Name], reaching out regarding a premium asset: **[Domain.com]**. Given your portfolio's focus on [Industry], this domain represents a significant opportunity for either a defensive play against competitors or a high-upside investment. I have the appraisal data and comparable sales ready if you'd like to review the numbers. Let me know if you're interested in the specifics."

## 4. Response Handling & Negotiation

### Common Objections
*   **"We're happy with our current domain."**
    *   *Response:* "Understood. Many of our clients use premium domains as 'redirect powerhouses' or for specific high-value campaigns to protect their brand. Is that something you've explored?"
*   **"The price is too high."**
    *   *Response:* "I appreciate the transparency. Premium domains are significant investments. What is the range you've budgeted for brand assets this year? We may be able to structure a lease-to-own or a performance-based payment plan."
*   **"Not interested."**
    *   *Response:* "Thanks for the quick reply, [Name]. Is there someone else on the team who handles brand acquisitions, or should I check back in 6 months?"

### Negotiation Best Practices
1.  **Always anchor high:** Start at the upper end of the valuation range.
2.  **Focus on the "Why Now":** Mention the scarcity of the asset.
3.  **The "Silent" Close:** Once a price is proposed, stop talking. Let them respond.

---

## 5. Seller Onboarding Process

To ensure high-quality campaigns, we require the following from every seller:
1.  **Proof of Ownership:** Screenshot of registrar dashboard or DNS TXT record.
2.  **Pricing Floor:** The absolute minimum (Reserve) and the "Happy Price" (Target).
3.  **Domain History:** Any traffic stats, age of domain, or previous offers received.
4.  **Authority to Negotiate:** A signed mandate (or digital agreement) allowing us to represent the domain for a set period (e.g., 90 days).
